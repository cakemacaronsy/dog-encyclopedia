FACTS — 04 CURATED
01 Nose Prints

A dog's nose print is as unique as a human fingerprint. The ridges and creases form a distinct pattern that can be used for identification — no two are alike in the entire world.

02 They Dream

Dogs experience REM sleep and dream much like humans do. That twitching and whimpering during sleep? They're likely chasing something — or being chased — through remembered landscapes.

03 300 Million Receptors

A dog's sense of smell is 40x more powerful than ours. They can detect cancer, predict seizures hours in advance, and identify a teaspoon of sugar dissolved in a million gallons of water.

04 Emotional Mirror

Dogs are the only non-primate animals that seek eye contact with humans. They evolved to read our faces and emotional states — a skill wolves, their ancestors, do not possess.
